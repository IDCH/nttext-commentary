What is this?

This enables you to see the New Testament through the eyes of ancient scribes.

It shows how information from the manuscripts reveals early understandings of the text.

Who is it for?

It’s for you! Both non-specialists and specialists who are engaged in study of the New Testament.

We designed it for those curious about the differences between their English Bibles and the underlying Greek text.

Start Your Search

To get started with the commentary, type your verse reference into the search box and click the green search button. During the preview phase the commentary is limited to searches in 1 Peter.

Jump back—or ahead

Once you’ve landed on a verse it’s easy to navigate to neighboring verses. The gray buttons on either side fo the search box let you navigate to next or previous verses and chapters. Single arrows move you by a verse and double arrows by a chapter.

Reading Your Text

The Bible Verse box displays your verse in parallel English and Greek versions. Any significant variants are highlighted and numbered consecutively (1, 2, 3, etc.) for easy reference between your verse’s variants and the textual commentary.
